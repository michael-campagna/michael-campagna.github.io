hi,

my name's Michael. I'm an undergraduate student at the College of William & Mary in Virginia, USA, where I spend most of my time pursuing my interest in mathematics and physics. My hopeful environmental vision and fascination with turbulent phenomena together manifest as a broad interest in theoretical and computational plasma physics and fusion science. My current research interests include:

  • turbulence and transport in magnetically confined plasmas
  • stellarator optimization, symmetries in nonaxisymmetric configurations
  • instability and disruption prediction and suppression in tokamaks
  • device simulation and modeling
  • magnetic reconnection (in both fusion and space plasmas)

Visit my projects page (currently under construction) for information about the sort of things I've worked on. In my spare time I like to sit outside and walk between trees. I also tutor math and physics and volunteer as a teacher's assistant for the Computational Research Access NEtwork (CRANE). Thank you for reading my website :)
